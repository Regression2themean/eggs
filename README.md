# Regression 2 The Mean

A minimalist finance and philosophy website exploring timeless lessons on market cycles, bubbles, panic, and the psychology of money.

## Brand Philosophy

**Headline:** "Markets change. Human behavior doesn't."

**Vision:** Timeless lessons on bubbles, cycles, panic, euphoria, and the psychology of money.

**Tone:** Not doom. Not hype. A calm, statistical perspective on human behavior and markets.

## Design System

### Colors
- **Background:** White (#ffffff)
- **Text:** Dark Charcoal (#2c2c2c)
- **Accent:** Muted Olive Green (#9b9870)
- **Hover:** Deep Olive (#7a7a5a)
- **Border:** Light Gray (#f0f0f0)
- **Secondary BG:** Off-white (#fafaf8)

### Typography
- **Body:** Inter (sans-serif)
- **Headings:** Merriweather (serif)
- **Line Height:** 1.6 for body, 1.2-1.4 for headings

### Spacing
Minimalist grid-based spacing system for clean, organized layout.

## Core Concepts

1. **Mean Reversion** - Extremes don't last. Prices, valuations, and sentiment always drift back toward average.
2. **The Psychology Trap** - Our brains are wired for survival, not wealth. We chase gains, fear losses, and trade irrationally during extremes.
3. **Cycles Within Cycles** - Bull and bear markets, business cycles, sentiment swings. Recognizing patterns doesn't predict timing—it prevents panic.
4. **Discipline Over Prediction** - You can't time markets. You can develop systems, stick to principles, and let compounding do the work.
